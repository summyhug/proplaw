Tab 01 — North Star

MVP Definition

Core Hypothesis

Germany's building law is fragmented across 16 sovereign state codes (LBO). Architects, planners, foreign investors and Bauherren have no unified, queryable interface to navigate this complexity — especially across language barriers.

Our differentiator: A bilingual (DE/EN) AI advisor combining FAISS vector retrieval and a NetworkX knowledge graph to surface precise, cross-referenced answers from all 16 LBOs — with transparent source attribution and explicit out-of-scope detection.

Target Users
Primary

Architects & Planners

Fast cross-Bundesland LBO comparison. International firms often work in English. High query complexity — exactly where KG outperforms RAG.

Primary

Bauherren & Developers

Need Abstandsflächen, GFZ, Bauklasse rules before engaging consultants. State-specific answers with explicit attribution.

Secondary

Foreign Investors

Evaluating development across German states. English-first. Benefit most from bilingual interface and cross-Bundesland comparison.

Secondary

Municipalities & Officials

Compare own LBO to neighbouring states. German-first. Precise §§ lookup during planning approvals.

Out of Scope

General Tenants

Wohnraummietrecht / WEG = Phase 2. Out-of-scope detection node redirects appropriately.

Tab 03 — Technical Differentiator

Knowledge Graph Architecture

Owner: Sumit (build) · Nuria (support) · restored from v5.1
Why KG — The Pitch Argument

Standard vector RAG retrieves semantically similar text — but LBO §§ are a network of interdependencies. § 6 (Abstandsflächen) only makes sense alongside § 7 (Bebauung) and § 5 (Bebauungsplan). A pure FAISS system returns § 6 and misses § 7 entirely — a critical omission in a legal context. A knowledge graph layer explicitly models these relationships, enabling the agent to traverse connected §§ and return complete, omission-resistant answers. This is our architectural differentiator and fulfils the bootcamp model comparison requirement.

Dual Retrieval Pipeline
Track A — RAG (Safety Net)
FAISS vector index → semantic similarity search → top-k chunks → LLM synthesis → citation output.

Owner: Sebastian
Deadline: Working end of Week 1
Fallback: If KG fails, RAG ships alone and is still a strong product
Track B — KG-Enhanced (Differentiator)
FAISS retrieves seed §§ → NetworkX graph traversal finds related nodes → expanded context sent to LLM → omission-resistant answer.

Owner: Sumit + Nuria
Deadline: Proof-of-concept end Week 2
Demo: Side-by-side comparison in pitch
Router logic: LangGraph node decides which track based on query complexity. Simple factual → RAG. Multi-§§, multi-party, or cross-Bundesland → KG-enhanced.

  "is_enrichment": false
}
